SATURN

OVERVIEW

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest planet in our solar system.

Adorned with a dazzling, complex system of icy rings, Saturn is unique in our solar system.
The other giant planets have rings, but none are as spectacular as Saturn's.

Like fellow gas giant Jupiter, Saturn is a massive ball made mostly of hydrogen and helium.

INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT SATURN

Nine Earths side by side would almost span Saturn’s diameter. That doesn’t include Saturn’s rings.

Saturn has 53 known moons with an additional 29 moons awaiting confirmation of their discovery—that
is a total of 82 moons.

Saturn takes about 10.7 hours (no one knows precisely) to rotate on its axis once—a Saturn “day”—and 29 Earth years to orbit the sun.
